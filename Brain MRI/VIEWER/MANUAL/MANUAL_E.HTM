1            About DICOM Viewer

 

The DICOM Viewer is a viewer program only for Hitachi MRI that conforms to DICOM standards. It can be used to display image data onto a computer from an archive CD that has been created with the CD-R archive function.

3            Starting the DICOM Viewer

 

After turning on the computer, insert the archive CD into the drive. The License Agreement for the DICOM Viewer is automatically shown on the screen. Carefully read the License Agreement, and click the [ACCEPT] button if you accept the terms of the license. The DICOM Viewer starts. If you do not accept the terms of the license, click the [DECLINE] button.

Once the DICOM Viewer starts, the Viewer window and Patient Selector dialog box appear and display images of the first series.

The size of the Viewer window is fixed, and cannot be adjusted. Move both the Viewer window and the Patient Selector dialog box to a position where they are easily viewable.

 

Depending on your computer’s settings (if the CD auto run setting has not been selected), the DICOM Viewer may not automatically start when the computer is turned on. In that case, open My Computer and double-click the drive in which you inserted the archive CD.

4            Displaying an image

 

To display an image, first click the  to the left of the patient folder on the left side of the Patient Selector dialog box to display the list of study folders. Then either click the study folder in this list that you want to display, or double-click the study that you want to display in the list view on the right side of the Patient Selector dialog box. 

The series included in the study selected in the list view is displayed.

Since the image data for the examination file of only one study for one patient is recorded on an archive CD, one patient folder and one study folder are displayed.

Next, click the series you want to display. All of the images included in the series are loaded and displayed.

To display a different series, click it in the list view of the Patient Selector dialog box.

5            Loading single images

 

From the menu bar, click [File] and then choose [Open DICOM file] to display the Open dialog box.

Fig.4 Open dialog box (single image)

 

Select a file and click the [Open] button to view that image in the viewer. You can select multiple images by holding down the [Ctrl] key while clicking. Then, click the [Open] button to display the multiple selected images in the viewer.

Also, to display the image that falls within the overlap between two selected images, hold the [Shift] key while selecting two image files and then click the [Open] button.

 

6            Loading images from a different drive

 

Use the following procedure to load image data from an archive CD or archive DVD-RAM inserted in a different CD-ROM or DVD-RAM drive.

From the menu bar, click [File] and then choose [Open DICOMDIR file] to display the Open dialog box. Select the "DICOMDIR" file on the archive CD or archive DVD-RAM that you want to display.

Fig.5 Open dialog box (images from a different drive)

 

Click the [Open] button to display the Patient Selector dialog box. If you select the series you want to display from the same Patient Selector dialog box that is normally displayed when the program is run, the images included in the series are displayed in the viewer.

7            Scrolling images

If the number of images loaded is greater than the number that can be displayed on the viewer, you can scroll the images to view those that are not displayed.

Use the scroll bar on the right edge of the Viewer window to scroll through the images. You can click either the  and  buttons, or drag the slider. One click of the  and  buttons scrolls the display by one image. You can also use the mouse wheel to scroll through the images.

19          Copying a window

 

The DICOM Viewer does not include functions for printing images or outputting image data.

To print images or save data, display the image you want to copy on the window, and then press either the [Print Screen] button (to copy all windows) or the [Alt] and [Print Screen] buttons (to copy the active window) on the keyboard. ([Alt] and [Print Screen] means to press the [Alt] and [Print Screen] buttons simultaneously.) The images captured on the window are copied to the clipboard. From there, they can be transferred to Paint or a similar image-processing program.
